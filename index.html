Who were the users?

What were their goals?

Gaming machine, High-end computer

What was the intended context or environment of this technology?

Supports two high-resolution graphic modes, smooth scrolling, "sprites", bit mapping, character collision resolution and character mapped graphics, and three channels of complex sound. Excellent game machine with thousands of software titles release and various peripherals to extend its capabilities.

What was unique or new about it?

Had new versions of the SID, VIC, and I/O chips, The vore voltage was reduced from 12V to 9V, Printed on the top of keys were the graphic symbols

Who developed the technology and what was their story?

Commodore International, January 1982 first shown at the Consumer Electronics Show, in Las Vegas, January 7–10, 1982 Volume production started in early 1982, marketing in August for US$595 (equivalent to $1,509 in 2017). Commodore produced many of its parts in-house to control costs, including custom integrated circuit chips from MOS Technology. It has been compared to the Ford Model T automobile for its role in bringing a new technology to middle-class households via creative and affordable mass-production. In 2011, 17 years after it was taken off the market, research showed that brand recognition for the model was still at 87%, Commodore International founded by Jack Tramiel, in 1954, Eventually the company went bankrupt
